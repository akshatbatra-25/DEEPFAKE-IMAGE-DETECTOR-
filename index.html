What are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes are synthetic media in which a person in an existing image or video is replaced with someone else's likeness using artificial neural networks. The term "deepfake" is a combination of "deep learning" and "fake," referring to the AI technology used to create these manipulations.

These AI-generated forgeries have become increasingly sophisticated and harder to detect with the naked eye. They pose significant threats to personal privacy, national security, and the integrity of information in our digital age.

Our cutting-edge detection system uses state-of-the-art deep learning algorithms to analyze facial features, expressions, and subtle inconsistencies that are often invisible to human observers. We help protect individuals, organizations, and society from the dangers of manipulated media.
